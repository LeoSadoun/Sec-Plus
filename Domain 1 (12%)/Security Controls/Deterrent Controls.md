### Official Definition
Security measures designed to discourage potential attackers from attempting a violation by influencing their perception of risk, effort, or consequence.

### Key Characteristics
- **Psychological:** Relies on the mindset of the attacker.
- **Passive:** Does not necessarily block the action, but makes it less attractive.
- **Visible:** Most effective when highly visible to the target audience.

### Real-World Examples
1. **"No Trespassing" Signs:** Warning individuals of legal consequences.
2. **Warning Banners:** Login screens stating that activity is monitored.
3. **Dummy Cameras:** Fake CCTV cameras that create the illusion of surveillance.
4. **Security Lighting:** Increasing the visibility of an attacker to discourage them.
5. **Uniformed Guards:** Their presence alone serves as a deterrent.

### Exam Tips
- **Keywords:** "Discourage," "Psychological," "Influence behavior," "Warning," "Perception."
- **Pitfall:** A deterrent control is NOT guaranteed to stop an attacker. A determined attacker will bypass it.
- **Interaction:** Deterrents work best when backed up by Preventive and Detective controls.

### Relationship to other controls
- **Physical:** A fence can be both Preventive (stops entry) and Deterrent (looks hard to climb).
- **Managerial:** A policy stating "All violations will be prosecuted" is a deterrent.
