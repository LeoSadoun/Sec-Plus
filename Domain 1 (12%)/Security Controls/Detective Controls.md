### Official Definition
Security measures that identify and record security incidents, violations, or unauthorized activities as they occur or after they have happened.

### Key Characteristics
- **Reactive/Active Monitoring:** Provides visibility into the security state.
- **Audit-Focused:** Generates logs and records for later review.
- **Trigger-Based:** Often alerts a person or system to take action.

### Real-World Examples
1. **Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS):** Alerting on suspicious network patterns.
2. **Log Review/SIEM:** Analyzing system logs for anomalies.
3. **CCTV Recording:** Reviewing footage after an event has occurred.
4. **Motion Sensors:** Triggering an alarm when movement is detected.
5. **Financial Audits:** Identifying discrepancies in accounting records.

### Exam Tips
- **Keywords:** "Identify," "Alert," "Notify," "Log," "Monitor," "Audit," "After the fact."
- **Pitfall:** Detective controls do NOT stop an attack; they only tell you that it is happening or has happened.
- **Interaction:** Detective controls are the "eyes" that trigger **Corrective** or **Incident Response** actions.

### Relationship to other controls
- **Technical:** An IDS is a technical detective control.
- **Physical:** A smoke detector is a physical detective control.
- **Operational:** A supervisor reviewing employee logs is an operational detective control.
