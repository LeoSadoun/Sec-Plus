### Official Definition
Security measures that specify required behavior, dictate actions, or mandate compliance with specific rules and regulations.

### Key Characteristics
- **Mandated:** Defines what "must" or "should" be done.
- **Prescriptive:** Provides specific directions to be followed.
- **Regulatory:** Often driven by law, industry standards, or company policy.

### Real-World Examples
1. **Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):** Directing employees on how they are allowed to use company resources.
2. **"Exit" and "Danger" Signs:** Directing physical movement and safety behavior.
3. **Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs):** Step-by-step instructions for performing tasks securely.
4. **Privacy Regulations (GDPR/HIPAA):** Directing how sensitive data must be handled.
5. **Security Banners:** Legally directing users that their activity is subject to monitoring.

### Exam Tips
- **Keywords:** "Must," "Required," "Dictate," "Direct," "Shall," "Mandatory," "Signage."
- **Pitfall:** Directive controls are often the legal or formal basis for other controls.
- **Interaction:** A Directive control (Policy) establishes the requirement that other controls (Technical/Physical) must fulfill.

### Relationship to other controls
- **Managerial:** Most policies are both Managerial and Directive.
- **Physical:** Safety signs are physical directive controls.
- **Operational:** Training sessions direct employees on secure practices.
