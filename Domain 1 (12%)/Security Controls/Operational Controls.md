### Official Definition
Security controls that are primarily implemented and executed by people (rather than systems) as part of day-to-day human activities and processes.

### Key Characteristics
- **Human-Centric:** Depends on individuals following established processes.
- **Process-Driven:** Focuses on the "how-to" of security maintenance.
- **Tactical:** Deals with immediate, routine security tasks.
- **Auditable:** Human actions are often recorded in logs or sign-in sheets.

### Real-World Examples
1. **Incident Response:** The human-led process of identifying and containing a security breach.
2. **Patch Management:** The manual or managed process of applying updates to systems.
3. **Physical Security Guards:** Personnel monitoring entrances and patrolling facilities.
4. **Change Management:** Human-reviewed processes for altering system configurations.
5. **Media Sanitation:** The physical destruction or wiping of hard drives by staff.

### Exam Tips
- **Keywords:** "Process," "Human activity," "Maintenance," "Lifecycle," "Implementation," "Daily tasks."
- **Pitfall:** Often confused with Managerial controls. Remember: Managerial = *Deciding/Planning*; Operational = *Doing/Executing*.
- **Interaction:** Operational controls (like Patching) are often the human execution of a Managerial requirement.

### Relationship to other controls
- **Preventive:** A security guard (Operational) prevents unauthorized entry.
- **Corrective:** Incident Response (Operational) corrects the damage from a breach.
- **Deterrent:** The presence of a guard (Operational) may deter an attacker.
