### Official Definition
Security measures implemented to mitigate damage, remediate vulnerabilities, and restore systems to a secure state after a security incident has been detected.

### Key Characteristics
- **Remedial:** Focuses on "fixing" the problem.
- **Recovery-Oriented:** Aims to return to the "last known good" state.
- **Reactive:** Always occurs *after* a detective control has signaled an issue.

### Real-World Examples
1. **Data Backups:** Restoring data after a ransomware attack or hardware failure.
2. **Antivirus Cleaning:** Removing or quarantining a file after it is flagged as malicious.
3. **Patching:** Fixing a vulnerability that was exploited during a breach.
4. **Intrusion Prevention Systems (IPS):** Automatically changing firewall rules to block an active attack.
5. **Business Continuity Plans:** Executing procedures to maintain operations during a disaster.

### Exam Tips
- **Keywords:** "Restore," "Fix," "Remedy," "Limit damage," "Clean up," "Recover."
- **Pitfall:** Sometimes confused with Preventive. IPS is **Corrective** when it acts *in response* to a detection to stop further damage.
- **Interaction:** Corrective controls depend on the accuracy and speed of **Detective** controls.

### Relationship to other controls
- **Technical:** IPS and Antivirus are technical corrective controls.
- **Operational:** Incident Response is an operational corrective control.
- **Managerial:** A Disaster Recovery Plan is a managerial corrective control.
