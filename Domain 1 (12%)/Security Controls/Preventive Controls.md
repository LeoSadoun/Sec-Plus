### Official Definition
Proactive security measures designed to stop a security incident or violation before it occurs by blocking unauthorized actions.

### Key Characteristics
- **Proactive:** Acts before the threat is realized.
- **Automated or Manual:** Can be a firewall (Technical) or a lock (Physical).
- **Hardening:** Often involves reducing the attack surface.

### Real-World Examples
1. **Firewalls:** Blocking unauthorized ports and IP addresses.
2. **Security Awareness Training:** Preventing social engineering by educating users.
3. **Locks and Fencing:** Preventing physical intrusion.
4. **Encryption:** Preventing data breaches by making data unreadable to unauthorized users.
5. **Least Privilege:** Preventing unauthorized access by limiting user rights.

### Exam Tips
- **Keywords:** "Stop," "Block," "Avoid," "Thwart," "Before," "Proactive."
- **Pitfall:** Don't confuse with Deterrent. A Deterrent *discourages*; a Preventive *stops*.
- **Interaction:** If a Preventive control fails, a Detective control should catch the event.

### Relationship to other controls
- **Technical:** A Firewall is a technical preventive control.
- **Physical:** A Bollard is a physical preventive control.
- **Managerial:** A Background Check is a managerial preventive control.
