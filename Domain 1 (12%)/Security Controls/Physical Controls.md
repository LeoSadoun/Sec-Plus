### Official Definition
Tangible security measures designed to physically restrict, detect, or prevent unauthorized access to facilities, equipment, or personnel.

### Key Characteristics
- **Tangible:** Can be seen, touched, or physically felt.
- **Environmental:** Focuses on the physical perimeter and internal facility areas.
- **Safety-First:** Often integrated with life-safety systems (e.g., fire suppression).

### Real-World Examples
1. **Fencing and Gates:** Physical barriers to define the perimeter.
2. **Locks and Biometric Scanners:** Restricting entry to doors and server racks.
3. **CCTV/Surveillance:** Visual monitoring of facility areas.
4. **Lighting:** Eliminating dark areas where intruders could hide.
5. **Bollards:** Physical pillars to prevent vehicle-borne attacks.

### Exam Tips
- **Keywords:** "Facility," "Perimeter," "Barrier," "Environmental," "Tangible," "Hardware (physical)."
- **Pitfall:** A "Biometric Scanner" is both a Technical control (the logic) and a Physical control (the hardware on the wall). In exams, focus on the context of the question.
- **Interaction:** Physical controls are often the first layer of "Defense in Depth."

### Relationship to other controls
- **Preventive:** A lock (Physical) prevents access.
- **Detective:** A motion sensor (Physical) detects movement.
- **Deterrent:** High fences (Physical) deter casual intruders.
