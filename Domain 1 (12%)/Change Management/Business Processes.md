## Official Definition
The formal process for planning, approving, implementing, and documenting changes to an IT environment to minimize risk and unintended consequences.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Request for Change (RFC):** The formal document that initiates the process, detailing what is being changed and why.
*   **Change Advisory Board (CAB):** A group of stakeholders who review, prioritize, and approve or reject changes.
*   **Impact Analysis:** Evaluating how a change will affect security, performance, and other systems.
*   **Backout Plan (Rollback Plan):** A documented procedure to revert the system to its previous state if the change fails.
*   **Maintenance Window:** A pre-defined, scheduled timeframe for implementing changes to minimize user impact.
*   **Approval Process:** Ensuring that the right level of management has signed off on the change.
*   **Standard Operating Procedure (SOP):** Documented steps to ensure the change is implemented consistently and securely.

## Real-World Examples
*   **Patch Management:** Following a change process before deploying OS security patches to production servers.
*   **Firewall Rule Changes:** Requiring an RFC and CAB approval before opening a new port on the network.
*   **Cloud Migration:** Using a formal process to move workloads from on-premises to AWS or Azure.

## Exam Tips
*   **CAB:** The group that approves changes.
*   **Backout Plan:** ALWAYS required; it's the "safety net."
*   **RFC:** The starting point of any formal change.
*   **Goal:** To minimize **Risk** and prevent **Downtime**.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Risk Management:** Change management is a critical risk mitigation strategy.
*   **Availability:** Prevents outages caused by poorly planned changes.
*   **Asset Management:** Ensures the asset inventory stays accurate as systems change.
