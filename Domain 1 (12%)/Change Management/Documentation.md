## Official Definition
The practice of maintaining accurate, up-to-date records of all changes, configurations, and procedures within an IT environment.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Network Diagrams:** Visual representations of the network topology, including routers, switches, and firewalls.
*   **Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs):** Step-by-step instructions for performing routine and emergency tasks.
*   **Inventory Management:** A record of all hardware and software assets.
*   **Change Logs:** A historical record of every change made to a system (who, what, when, why).
*   **Policies and Procedures:** High-level rules and the specific steps taken to follow them.
*   **Physical Diagrams:** Documenting the physical location of servers, cables, and rack layouts.

## Real-World Examples
*   **Wiki/Confluence:** Centralized internal documentation for IT teams.
*   **CMDB (Configuration Management Database):** A database used to store information about configuration items (CIs) and their relationships.
*   **README files:** Technical documentation included with code or configuration scripts.

## Exam Tips
*   **Audit Trail:** Documentation provides the evidence needed for compliance audits (e.g., SOC2, PCI).
*   **Troubleshooting:** Accurate diagrams and logs are the first things needed during an outage.
*   **Knowledge Transfer:** Ensures that when an employee leaves, their knowledge remains with the organization.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Compliance:** Many regulations (GDPR, HIPAA) mandate strict documentation practices.
*   **Business Continuity:** Essential for rebuilding systems from scratch during a disaster.
*   **Incident Response:** Documentation helps responders understand what the "normal" state of the system should be.
