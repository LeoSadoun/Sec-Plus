## Official Definition
Broad strategies and governance principles that apply to the lifecycle of data within an organization.

## Key Characteristics/Technical Details
*   **Data Sovereignty:** The concept that data is subject to the laws of the country in which it is physically located.
*   **Data Retention:** Policies defining how long data must be kept and when it must be destroyed (to minimize liability).
*   **Data Minimization:** Collecting and keeping only the data that is absolutely necessary for a specific purpose.
*   **Data Disposal:** Securely destroying data so it cannot be recovered (Shredding, Burning, Degaussing, Wiping/Purging).
*   **Data Roles:**
    *   **Owner:** Ultimate responsibility for the data (usually a senior executive).
    *   **Controller:** Determines the purpose and means of processing (GDPR term).
    *   **Processor:** Processes data on behalf of the controller (e.g., a cloud provider).
    *   **Custodian/Steward:** Responsible for technical tasks like backups and storage.

## Real-World Examples
*   **Data Sovereignty:** An EU company using a US-based cloud provider must ensure data stays on servers located within the EU to comply with GDPR.
*   **Data Retention:** A bank keeping loan applications for 7 years after the loan is paid off, as required by law.
*   **Degaussing:** Using a powerful magnet to destroy data on a magnetic hard drive before disposal.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** Sovereignty, Retention, Minimization, Degaussing, Purging, Custodian.
*   **Scenario:** What is the most important factor in where data is stored? Data Sovereignty (Jurisdiction/Laws).
*   **Scenario:** Who is responsible for backing up the data? The Custodian.
*   **Scenario:** How to dispose of an SSD? Wiping/Crypto-erase (Degaussing doesn't work on SSDs).

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Governance:** These considerations are part of the overall GRC (Governance, Risk, and Compliance) framework.
*   **Liability:** Proper retention and disposal reduce the organization's legal and financial risk.
