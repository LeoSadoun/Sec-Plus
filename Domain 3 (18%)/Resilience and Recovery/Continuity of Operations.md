## Official Definition
A collection of processes and procedures that ensure an organization can continue to function during and after a disaster.

## Key Characteristics/Technical Details
*   **BIA (Business Impact Analysis):** The process of identifying critical functions and the impact of their failure.
*   **RTO (Recovery Time Objective):** The maximum amount of time a system can be down after a disaster (Target time to resume).
*   **RPO (Recovery Point Objective):** The maximum age of files that must be recovered (How much data loss is acceptable).
*   **MTBF (Mean Time Between Failures):** Predicted elapsed time between inherent failures of a system during operation.
*   **MTTR (Mean Time to Repair):** The average time required to repair a failed component or device.
*   **Mission-Essential Functions:** The "must-haves" that the business cannot survive without for any length of time.

## Real-World Examples
*   **RPO:** A company decides they can only afford to lose 4 hours of data. Therefore, they must perform backups every 4 hours.
*   **BIA:** Determining that the website checkout process is a "Mission-Essential Function," while the employee holiday booking portal is not.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** BIA, RTO, RPO, MTBF, MTTR, Continuity of Operations Plan (COOP).
*   **Scenario:** If a business needs to be back online within 2 hours, what is that called? RTO.
*   **Scenario:** If a business can afford to lose up to 1 day's worth of data, what is that called? RPO.
*   **Key Concept:** BCP (Business Continuity Planning) is the broad umbrella; DRP (Disaster Recovery Planning) is the technical subset for IT.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Resilience:** Continuity planning is the strategic level of resilience.
*   **Risk Management:** BIA is a critical input to the risk management process.
*   **Availability:** Ensures the long-term "A" in CIA even in the event of major disasters.
