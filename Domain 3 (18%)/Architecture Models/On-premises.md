## Official Definition
A traditional computing model where an organization maintains its own physical servers, storage, and networking hardware within its own data center or facility.

## Key Characteristics/Technical Details
*   **Full Control:** Organization owns and manages the entire stack from physical hardware to applications.
*   **Capital Expenditure (CapEx):** High upfront costs for hardware and infrastructure.
*   **Maintenance:** Responsibility for power, cooling, physical security, and hardware lifecycle management.
*   **Air-Gapped Potential:** Easier to implement physically isolated networks for high-security requirements.
*   **Data Sovereignty:** Direct control over where data is stored and how it is accessed.

## Real-World Examples
*   **Legacy Systems:** Older applications that cannot be easily migrated to the cloud.
*   **Highly Regulated Industries:** Finance or healthcare organizations keeping sensitive data on-site to meet strict compliance requirements.
*   **Private Data Centers:** Large enterprises maintaining their own server farms.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** CapEx, total control, physical security, air-gap.
*   **Scenario:** If an organization needs absolute control over the physical hardware and location of data, choose On-premises.
*   **Contrast:** Often compared to Cloud (OpEx) regarding cost and scalability.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **CIA Triad:** Provides high **Confidentiality** and **Integrity** through physical isolation, but **Availability** may be lower compared to cloud due to reliance on local power and hardware redundancy.
*   **Physical Controls:** Requires robust physical security (locks, cameras, guards) which are managed by the cloud provider in cloud models.
