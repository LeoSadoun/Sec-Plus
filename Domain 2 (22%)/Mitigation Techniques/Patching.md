## Official Definition
* **Patching:** The process of applying updates to software and firmware to fix security vulnerabilities, bugs, or to add new features.

## Key Characteristics/Technical Details
* **Security Update:** Specifically addresses a security vulnerability.
* **Critical Update:** Addresses a significant bug or vulnerability that affects system stability or security.
* **Hotfix:** A small, quick update designed to fix a very specific issue.
* **Service Pack:** A large collection of updates and hotfixes rolled into one.
* **Patch Management Lifecycle:** Identification, Testing, Deployment, and Validation.
* **Emergency Out-of-Band (OOB) Patches:** Released outside of the normal schedule to address critical, zero-day vulnerabilities.

## Real-World Examples
* **Patch Tuesday:** Microsoft's regular schedule (second Tuesday of the month) for releasing security updates.
* **Equifax:** Failed to apply a patch for a known vulnerability (Apache Struts) for over two months, leading to a massive data breach.

## Exam Tips
* **Keywords:** Patch management, Testing, Zero-day, Legacy systems, Vulnerability, Lifecycle.
* **Scenario:** If a system is "vulnerable to a known exploit," the primary mitigation is patching. **Always test patches before deployment in production.**

## Relationship to other concepts
* **Vulnerabilities:** The primary defense against OS-based, Application, and Web-based vulnerabilities.
* **Hardening:** Patching is a core component of the hardening process.
