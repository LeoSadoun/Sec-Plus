## Official Definition
* **Message-based Vector:** A threat vector that utilizes communication platforms—such as email, SMS, or instant messaging—to deliver malicious payloads or conduct social engineering.

## Key Characteristics/Technical Details
* **Phishing:** Broad, non-targeted email campaigns designed to trick users into revealing credentials or installing malware.
* **Spear Phishing:** Highly targeted phishing aimed at a specific individual or group, often using personal details to build trust.
* **Whaling:** Spear phishing targeted at high-level executives (CEOs/CFOs).
* **Smishing:** Phishing via SMS (text messages).
* **Vishing:** Phishing via Voice calls (often automated or using deepfake audio).
* **Spam:** Unsolicited bulk messages, which may contain links to malicious sites or attachments.

## Real-World Examples
* **Ubiquiti Networks BEC (2015):** A Business Email Compromise (BEC) attack where scammers impersonated executives via email to trick the finance department into transferring $46 million.
* **Twilio Smishing Attack (2022):** Employees were targeted with SMS messages claiming their passwords had expired, leading to a significant breach.

## Exam Tips
* **Keywords:** Phishing, Smishing, Vishing, Whaling, BEC, Attachments, Malicious links.
* **Scenario:** If a user receives an "urgent email from the CEO" or a "text about a package delivery" that requires clicking a link, it's a message-based vector.

## Relationship to other concepts
* **Social Engineering:** The primary technique used in message-based attacks.
* **Malicious Activity:** Often leads to Malware or Password attacks.
* **Mitigation:** DMARC/SPF/DKIM, Email filtering, and Security Awareness Training.
