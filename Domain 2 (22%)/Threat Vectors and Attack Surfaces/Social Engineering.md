## Official Definition
* **Social Engineering:** The psychological manipulation of people into performing actions or divulging confidential information. It exploits human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities.

## Key Characteristics/Technical Details
* **Pretexting:** Creating a fabricated scenario (the pretext) to steal information (e.g., pretending to be an IT auditor).
* **Baiting:** Leaving a malware-infected physical device (like a USB drive) in a public place for someone to find and plug in.
* **Tailgating / Piggybacking:** Following an authorized person into a secure area without their knowledge (tailgating) or with their consent (piggybacking).
* **Quid Pro Quo:** Offering a service or benefit in exchange for information (e.g., "I'm from IT, I can fix your slow computer if you give me your password").
* **Shoulder Surfing:** Looking over someone's shoulder to see their screen or keypad.
* **Social Engineering Principles:** Authority, Intimidation, Consensus (Social Proof), Scarcity, Urgency, Familiarity/Liking, Trust.

## Real-World Examples
* **2020 Twitter Hack:** Attackers used social engineering to gain access to internal Twitter tools by calling employees and pretending to be from the IT department.
* **Robinhood Breach (2021):** An attacker called a customer support representative and used social engineering to gain access to certain customer support systems.

## Exam Tips
* **Keywords:** Psychology, Manipulation, Pretext, Urgency, Authority, Baiting, Tailgating.
* **Scenario:** If an attack involves "trickery," "impersonation," or "exploiting human trust," it's social engineering.

## Relationship to other concepts
* **Threat Vectors:** Often the delivery mechanism for Message-based and Voice Call vectors.
* **Mitigation:** Security Awareness Training is the most effective defense.
