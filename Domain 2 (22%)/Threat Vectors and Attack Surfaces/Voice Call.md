## Official Definition
* **Voice Call Vector:** A threat vector that uses telephony and voice communication to conduct social engineering and fraud. Also known as **Vishing** (Voice Phishing).

## Key Characteristics/Technical Details
* **Caller ID Spoofing:** Manipulating the caller ID display to make a call appear as if it's coming from a trusted source (e.g., a bank or government agency).
* **Deepfake Audio:** Using AI-generated voices to impersonate specific individuals, such as a CEO or a family member.
* **IVR (Interactive Voice Response) Scams:** Using automated systems to prompt users to enter sensitive information like PINs or account numbers.
* **Intimidation:** Using high-pressure tactics (e.g., "The IRS is filing a lawsuit against you") to force immediate action.

## Real-World Examples
* **CEO Fraud via Deepfake (2019):** A UK energy firm CEO was tricked into transferring €220,000 after a caller used AI to perfectly mimic the voice of the parent company's chief executive.
* **IRS Scams:** Common automated calls claiming the recipient owes back taxes and must pay immediately via gift cards or wire transfers.

## Exam Tips
* **Keywords:** Vishing, Spoofing, Deepfake audio, IVR, Telephony, Intimidation.
* **Scenario:** An employee receives a call from "IT" asking for their password to "validate an account update."

## Relationship to other concepts
* **Social Engineering:** The underlying technique used in voice-based attacks.
* **Mitigation:** User training, verifying identity through out-of-band channels, and call filtering.
