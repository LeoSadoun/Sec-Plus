## Official Definition
* **Hacktivist:** An individual or group that uses cyberattacks as a form of social or political activism. Their primary goal is to promote a cause, expose perceived injustices, or embarrass an organization.

## Key Characteristics/Technical Details
* **Ideological Motivation:** Driven by political, social, environmental, or religious beliefs rather than financial gain.
* **Common Tactics:**
    * **DDoS:** Overwhelming a target's website to silence them or gain publicity.
    * **Doxing:** Stealing and releasing private information (PII) to damage reputations.
    * **Web Defacement:** Changing a target's website to display a political message.
* **Publicity Seekers:** Unlike APTs, hacktivists often want their actions to be known and publicized.

## Real-World Examples
* **Anonymous:** A decentralized collective known for various operations (e.g., Project Chanology against Scientology, operations against government censorship).
* **WikiLeaks:** While not an attacker in the traditional sense, it is often associated with the hacktivist ethos of "information freedom."
* **LulzSec:** A spin-off of Anonymous that targeted Sony, the CIA, and others for "the lulz" but with political undertones.

## Exam Tips
* **Keywords:** Activism, Social cause, Political motivation, Publicity, Doxing, Defacement.
* **Scenario:** If an attack is described as being "in retaliation for a company policy" or to "expose government corruption," it's Hacktivism.

## Relationship to other concepts
* **Motivations:** Primarily Ideological/Political.
* **Threat Vectors:** Often uses Social Engineering or Message-based vectors to gather information.
