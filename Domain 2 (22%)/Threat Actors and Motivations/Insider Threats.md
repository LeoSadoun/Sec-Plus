## Official Definition
* **Insider Threat:** A threat to an organization that comes from people within the organization, such as employees, former employees, contractors, or business associates, who have inside information concerning the organization's security practices, data, and computer systems.

## Key Characteristics/Technical Details
* **Trust and Access:** The primary danger is that they already have legitimate credentials and authorized access to the network.
* **Types of Insiders:**
    * **Malicious Insider:** Intentionally steals data or sabotages systems for personal gain, revenge, or ideological reasons.
    * **Negligent Insider:** Unintentionally causes a breach through poor security habits (e.g., leaving a laptop in a car, clicking a phishing link).
    * **Accidental Insider:** Makes an honest mistake (e.g., misconfiguring a database to be public).
* **Detection Difficulty:** Harder to detect than external threats because their activity often blends in with normal business operations.

## Real-World Examples
* **Edward Snowden:** A contractor who used his authorized access to leak classified NSA documents.
* **Tesla Sabotage (2018):** An employee who modified the manufacturing OS and exported large amounts of sensitive data to third parties.
* **Waymo vs. Uber:** An engineer (Anthony Levandowski) who downloaded thousands of confidential files before leaving for a competitor.

## Exam Tips
* **Keywords:** Internal, Authorized access, Disgruntled employee, Privilege abuse, Negligence, Least Privilege.
* **Scenario:** Look for "unauthorized data transfer" by a "current employee" or "former employee whose access wasn't revoked."

## Relationship to other concepts
* **Mitigation:** Least Privilege, Role-Based Access Control (RBAC), Offboarding procedures, and UBA (User Behavior Analytics).
* **Fundamental Concepts:** Directly impacts Confidentiality and Integrity.
