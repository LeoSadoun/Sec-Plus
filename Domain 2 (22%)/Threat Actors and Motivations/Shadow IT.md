## Official Definition
* **Shadow IT:** The use of information technology systems, devices, software, applications, and services without explicit organizational approval. This is often driven by employees seeking to be more productive.

## Key Characteristics/Technical Details
* **Unmanaged Assets:** Includes personal cloud storage (Dropbox/Drive), unsanctioned SaaS (Trello/Slack), and personal mobile devices (BYOD).
* **Security Gaps:** These assets lack corporate security controls (encryption, monitoring, patching) and are often misconfigured.
* **Compliance Risk:** Can lead to data being stored in non-compliant locations, violating regulations like GDPR or HIPAA.
* **Visibility Issues:** IT and Security teams cannot protect what they don't know exists.

## Real-World Examples
* **Departmental Cloud Usage:** A marketing team setting up their own AWS instance to host a microsite because the IT ticket was taking too long.
* **SaaS Sprawl:** Employees using personal accounts for project management tools to collaborate with external vendors without vetting the tool's security.

## Exam Tips
* **Keywords:** Unsanctioned, Productivity, Lack of visibility, Unmanaged, SaaS, BYOD.
* **Scenario:** A user installs a "helpful productivity tool" or uses a "personal file-sharing account" without IT's knowledge.

## Relationship to other concepts
* **Threat Vectors:** Creates an expanded Attack Surface that is unmonitored.
* **Vulnerabilities:** Often leads to Cloud-specific or Application vulnerabilities due to misconfiguration.
* **Mitigation:** Cloud Access Security Brokers (CASB), regular network discovery, and clear security policies.
