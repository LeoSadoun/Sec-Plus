## Official Definition
* **Unskilled Attacker:** An individual with limited technical knowledge who uses pre-made tools, scripts, or programs developed by others to launch cyberattacks. Historically referred to as "Script Kiddies."

## Key Characteristics/Technical Details
* **Tool Reliance:** Uses automated scanners, exploit kits (e.g., Metasploit, Kali Linux tools), and DDoS-for-hire services.
* **Low Sophistication:** Lacks the ability to write custom code or discover new vulnerabilities; depends on known, unpatched exploits.
* **Visibility:** Attacks are often noisy and easily detected by basic security controls because they use standard, well-known signatures.
* **Motivation:** Often seeking attention, "bragging rights," or simple financial gain through low-effort means.

## Real-World Examples
* **DDoS Attacks:** Using "booter" or "stresser" websites to take down gaming servers or small business websites.
* **Mass Defacement:** Using automated scripts to find websites with known CMS vulnerabilities (like old WordPress versions) to change the homepage.

## Exam Tips
* **Keywords:** Script Kiddie, Pre-made tools, Low sophistication, Noisy, Automated scripts.
* **Scenario:** Look for descriptions of attackers who "lack technical skills" or "use programs they didn't write."

## Relationship to other concepts
* **Vulnerabilities:** Targets "Low Hanging Fruit" such as unpatched software and default configurations.
* **Security Controls:** Easily mitigated by standard Patching, Firewalls, and IPS.
