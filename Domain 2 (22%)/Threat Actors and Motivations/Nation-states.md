## Official Definition
* **Nation-state Actor:** A highly sophisticated, state-sponsored threat actor that conducts cyber operations to further national interests, such as espionage, political influence, or economic disruption. They are characterized by long-term persistence and significant resources.

## Key Characteristics/Technical Details
* **Advanced Persistent Threat (APT):** Often synonymous with nation-state activity; emphasizes long-term, stealthy presence in a network.
* **Resources:** Massive funding, specialized technical teams, and access to proprietary or non-public exploits (e.g., zero-days).
* **Focus:** Strategic targets including government agencies, critical infrastructure (energy, water), defense contractors, and high-tech industries.
* **Stealth:** Utilizes custom-built malware, advanced obfuscation, and "living off the land" (LotL) techniques to evade detection.

## Real-World Examples
* **Stuxnet:** A highly sophisticated worm (attributed to US/Israel) designed to physically damage Iran's nuclear enrichment centrifuges.
* **SolarWinds Supply Chain Attack:** Attributed to Russian-linked actors (APT29), targeting the software update mechanism to breach US government agencies.
* **Lazarus Group:** Attributed to North Korea; involved in the Sony Pictures hack and the WannaCry ransomware outbreak.

## Exam Tips
* **Keywords:** APT, Government-sponsored, Zero-day, Strategic, Long-term, Geopolitical.
* **Scenario:** If a question mentions a "highly sophisticated" attack targeting "intellectual property" or "critical infrastructure" with "unlimited funding," think Nation-state/APT.

## Relationship to other concepts
* **Threat Vectors:** Often uses Supply Chain or Vulnerable Software vectors.
* **Motivations:** Primarily Political, Espionage, or Strategic Advantage.
* **Security Controls:** Requires advanced detection (EDR/XDR) and rigorous segmentation.
