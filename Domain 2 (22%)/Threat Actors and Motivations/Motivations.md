## Official Definition
* **Motivations:** The underlying reasons why a threat actor chooses to launch an attack. Understanding motivation helps in profiling threat actors and predicting their targets and tactics.

## Key Characteristics/Technical Details
* **Financial:** Seeking money through theft, extortion (ransomware), or fraud. (Organized Crime, Insider Threats).
* **Ideological:** Driven by political, social, or religious beliefs. (Hacktivists).
* **Political/Espionage:** Seeking strategic advantage, intellectual property, or geopolitical influence. (Nation-states).
* **Revenge:** A disgruntled employee or former partner seeking to damage a reputation or sabotage systems. (Insider Threats).
* **Peer Recognition:** Seeking status or "bragging rights" within the hacker community. (Unskilled Attackers).
* **Economic Espionage:** Stealing trade secrets from a competitor to gain a market advantage.

## Real-World Examples
* **WannaCry:** Primarily financial (ransomware) but with geopolitical undertones (attributed to North Korea).
* **Target Breach:** Purely financial; stolen credit card data sold on the dark web.
* **Sony Pictures Hack:** Primarily revenge/political; to prevent the release of a film.

## Exam Tips
* **Keywords:** Financial, Political, Ideological, Revenge, Espionage, Strategic.
* **Scenario:** Pay attention to *why* the attack is happening. If it's about a "cause," it's ideological. If it's about "profit," it's financial.

## Relationship to other concepts
* **Threat Actors:** Each actor type is typically associated with specific motivations.
* **Risk Management:** Motivation affects the "Likelihood" and "Impact" components of risk assessment.
