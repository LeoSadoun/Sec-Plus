## Official Definition
* **Organized Crime:** A group of cybercriminals who coordinate their activities to conduct systematic, high-profit illegal acts. Their primary driver is almost always financial gain.

## Key Characteristics/Technical Details
* **Profit-Driven:** Focused on monetization through ransomware, data theft for sale (PII/Credit Cards), and business email compromise (BEC).
* **Division of Labor:** Often operates like a legitimate corporation with specialized roles (coders, recruiters, money launderers, customer support for ransomware).
* **Sophistication:** Can be highly sophisticated, sometimes purchasing zero-days or hiring top-tier talent.
* **Resilience:** Often operates in jurisdictions where they are protected from extradition, making them difficult to shut down.

## Real-World Examples
* **REvil / Sodinokibi:** A prolific ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS) group that targeted Kaseya and JBS.
* **Conti Group:** A Russian-based ransomware syndicate known for its aggressive tactics and corporate-like structure.
* **FIN7 / Carbanak:** A group specialized in stealing payment card data from retailers and hospitality businesses.

## Exam Tips
* **Keywords:** Financial gain, Ransomware, Money laundering, Corporate structure, Cybercrime-as-a-Service.
* **Scenario:** If an attack involves "encrypting data for a fee" or "selling customer data on the dark web," Organized Crime is the likely actor.

## Relationship to other concepts
* **Malicious Activity:** Frequently uses Ransomware and Password attacks.
* **Motivations:** Primarily Financial.
* **Mitigation:** Robust Backups, Cyber Insurance, and Endpoint Protection.
