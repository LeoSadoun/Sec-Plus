## Official Definition
* **Physical Attack:** An attack that involves physical access to a building, server room, or device to compromise security.

## Key Characteristics/Technical Details
* **Tailgating:** Following someone into a secure area.
* **Lock Picking:** Physically bypassing door or cabinet locks.
* **USB Rubber Ducky:** A malicious USB device that mimics a keyboard to inject commands into a computer.
* **Skimming:** Installing devices on ATMs or gas pumps to steal credit card data.
* **Dumpster Diving:** Searching through trash for sensitive information (passwords, memos, hardware).
* **Hardware Tampering:** Opening a device to install a bug or extract data directly from chips.
* **Theft:** Physically stealing laptops, servers, or backup tapes.

## Real-World Examples
* **Target Breach (Skimming):** While the main breach was digital, many retail breaches start with physical skimmers on POS terminals.
* **Stuxnet (USB):** The initial infection of the air-gapped nuclear facility is believed to have occurred via a physically planted USB drive.

## Exam Tips
* **Keywords:** Tailgating, Skimming, Dumpster diving, USB, Physical bypass, CCTV, Mantrap.
* **Scenario:** An attacker "finds a password in the trash" or "follows an employee through a secure door."

## Relationship to other concepts
* **Security Controls:** Mitigated by Physical Controls like locks, badges, mantraps, and cameras.
* **Social Engineering:** Often used to facilitate physical access.
