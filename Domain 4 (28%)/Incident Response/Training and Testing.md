## Official Definition
Activities designed to ensure that the IR team and the broader organization are prepared to respond effectively to a security incident.

## Key Mechanisms/Tools
*   **Tabletop Exercises (TTX):** A discussion-based drill where the team talks through their response to a hypothetical scenario.
*   **Walkthroughs:** A basic review of the IR plan and individual roles.
*   **Simulations (Parallel/Full):** A realistic drill where technical teams actually perform response actions in a lab or non-production environment.
*   **Blue Team vs. Red Team Drills:** Red team (Attackers) vs. Blue team (Defenders) to test real-world detection and response capabilities.
*   **Disaster Recovery (DR) Testing:** Verifying that backups work and that systems can be restored within the RTO/RPO limits.

## Real-World Examples
*   The executive team and IT staff meet for two hours to discuss how they would respond if the CEO's laptop was stolen (Tabletop Exercise).
*   A "Purple Team" exercise where the Red team explains their attack techniques to the Blue team so they can improve their detection rules.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** Tabletop, Simulation, Red/Blue team, RTO/RPO, walkthrough.
*   **Scenario:** Which exercise is the most cost-effective way to find gaps in a policy? **Tabletop Exercise**.
*   **Scenario:** Testing the actual *restoration* of data is part of **Disaster Recovery Testing**.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **IR Processes:** Training and testing are the core components of the "Preparation" phase.
*   **Automation and Orchestration:** Playbooks should be tested during simulations to ensure they work as expected.
*   **Computing Resources:** Monitoring tools are validated during Blue team exercises to see if they catch the Red team's activity.
