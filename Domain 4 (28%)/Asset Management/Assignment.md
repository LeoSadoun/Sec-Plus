## Official Definition
The formal process of documenting the ownership and responsibility of an asset to a specific individual or department.

## Key Mechanisms/Tools
*   **Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):** A document users must sign acknowledging their responsibilities when assigned an asset.
*   **Asset Tagging:** Physically labeling hardware with a unique ID linked to an owner in the database.
*   **Custody Tracking:** Maintaining a "Chain of Custody" for sensitive assets.
*   **Onboarding/Offboarding:** Automated workflows that assign assets during hiring and reclaim them during termination.

## Real-World Examples
*   An employee receives a laptop and signs an AUP stating they will not install unauthorized software.
*   Using an ITSM (IT Service Management) tool like ServiceNow to link a specific serial number to a user's employee ID.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** AUP, asset owner, custody, onboarding/offboarding.
*   **Scenario:** Who is ultimately responsible for the security of a specific dataset? The **Data Owner**.
*   **Scenario:** Ensuring an employee returns their laptop when they leave? The **Offboarding** process.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Identity and Access Management:** Asset assignment is often tied to a user's identity and roles.
*   **Incident Response:** Knowing who was assigned a device is critical for investigating internal threats.
*   **Legal/Compliance:** Asset assignment records are used to prove who had access to what during an audit.
