## Official Definition
The process of obtaining new hardware, software, or services, ensuring they meet security standards and are properly recorded in the asset inventory from the start.

## Key Mechanisms/Tools
*   **Supply Chain Security:** Evaluating vendors for security practices (e.g., ISO 27001 certification).
*   **Secure Procurement:** Including security requirements in SLAs (Service Level Agreements) and contracts.
*   **Hardware Root of Trust:** Ensuring new hardware includes a **TPM (Trusted Platform Module)** or similar secure element.
*   **Software Licensing:** Verifying licenses to avoid legal risk and ensuring the software is from a reputable source.

## Real-World Examples
*   A company requires all new laptops to have a TPM 2.0 chip and a pre-installed corporate "Golden Image."
*   Evaluating a SaaS vendor's SOC 2 Type II report before signing a multi-year service contract.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** Supply chain, vendor risk, SLA, TPM, procurement.
*   **Scenario:** How to prevent counterfeit hardware? Use trusted vendors and verify the supply chain.
*   **Scenario:** Secure acquisition starts with **Requirements Gathering**.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Computing Resources:** Acquisition is the first step in creating a secure baseline.
*   **Vulnerability Management:** New assets must be scanned for vulnerabilities immediately upon acquisition.
*   **Compliance:** Proper acquisition records are essential for audits and regulatory compliance.
