## Official Definition
The ongoing process of auditing and reviewing a vendor's security performance to ensure they remain compliant with agreed-upon standards.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Right-to-Audit Clause:** A contract provision allowing the organization to perform periodic security reviews of the vendor.
*   **Performance Metrics:** Reviewing SLA reports to ensure the vendor is meeting uptime and security requirements.
*   **Incident Notification:** Verifying that the vendor is reporting security incidents within the timeframe specified in the contract.
*   **Renewal Review:** Re-assessing a vendor's risk profile before renewing their contract.

## Real-World Examples
*   **Annual Review:** Requesting a fresh SOC 2 report every year from your cloud storage provider.
*   **SLA Credit:** Demanding a refund because a vendor's service was down for 12 hours, violating the SLA.
*   **Continuous Monitoring:** Using a service like BitSight or SecurityScorecard to monitor a vendor's external security posture.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Ongoing," "Audit," "SLA Review," "Notification," "Performance."
*   **Scenario:** A company checks every six months to ensure its cloud provider is still PCI DSS compliant. This is **Vendor Monitoring**.
*   **Lifecycle:** Security doesn't end when the contract is signed; it requires continuous oversight.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Compliance Monitoring:** Vendor monitoring ensures that third parties don't cause the organization to fall out of compliance.
*   **Risk Reporting:** Vendor security performance should be included in regular risk reports to management.
