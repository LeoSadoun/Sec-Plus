## Official Definition
Legal and technical documents that define the relationship, expectations, and security requirements between an organization and a third party.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **SLA (Service Level Agreement):** Defines specific performance metrics (e.g., 99.9% uptime) and penalties for failure.
*   **MOU (Memorandum of Understanding):** A non-binding agreement outlining a mutual goal or "gentleman's agreement."
*   **ISA (Interconnection Security Agreement):** Specifically defines the technical requirements for connecting two organizations' networks.
*   **BPA (Business Partnership Agreement):** A legal contract between partners that includes profit sharing and security responsibilities.
*   **NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement):** Protects sensitive information from being shared by the third party.

## Real-World Examples
*   **SLA:** An ISP promising a maximum of 4 hours of downtime per year.
*   **MOU:** Two local hospitals agreeing to share resources during a city-wide disaster.
*   **ISA:** A government agency defining the VPN tunnel parameters for a contractor to access their database.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "SLA (Uptime)," "MOU (Mutual Goal)," "ISA (Technical Connection)," "NDA (Confidentiality)."
*   **Distinction:** If you see "Technical details of a connection," look for **ISA**. If you see "Uptime/Availability," look for **SLA**.
*   **Binding:** SLAs and BPAs are typically binding; MOUs are often not.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Incident Response:** SLAs often include required "time to notify" after a breach is discovered.
*   **Data Protection:** NDAs are a foundational administrative control for confidentiality.
