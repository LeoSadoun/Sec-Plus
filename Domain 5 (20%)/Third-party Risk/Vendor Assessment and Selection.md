## Official Definition
The process of evaluating the security posture of potential vendors and choosing those that align with the organization's risk tolerance.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Due Diligence:** Investigating a vendor's security history, financial stability, and compliance before signing a contract.
*   **Risk Profile:** Determining the level of risk a vendor poses based on the data they will access.
*   **Supply Chain Risk:** The risk that a vendor’s own suppliers may be compromised.
*   **Conflict of Interest:** Ensuring that the selection process is fair and unbiased.

## Real-World Examples
*   **SOC 2 Report Review:** Reading a cloud provider's SOC 2 Type II report to verify their security controls are effective.
*   **Security Audit:** Conducting an on-site audit of a physical document shredding company.
*   **Proof of Concept (PoC):** Testing a security software’s performance in a lab environment before purchase.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Due Diligence," "Selection," "Risk Profile," "SOC 2," "Supply Chain."
*   **Scenario:** Before hiring a payroll provider, the security team reviews their encryption standards and incident response plan. This is **Due Diligence**.
*   **Evaluation:** Security should be a primary factor in the selection process, not just price.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Risk Management:** Vendor selection is a critical point for mitigating supply chain risk.
*   **Security Governance:** Policies should dictate the minimum security requirements for all third-party partners.
