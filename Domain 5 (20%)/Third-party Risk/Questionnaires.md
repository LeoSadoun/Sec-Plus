## Official Definition
Standardized sets of questions sent to vendors to gather information about their security controls, policies, and procedures.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Self-Assessment:** Vendors answer the questions themselves, which may introduce bias.
*   **Standardized Frameworks:** Often based on NIST, ISO, or the SIG (Standardized Information Gathering) questionnaire.
*   **Evidence Request:** Asking for documentation (e.g., screen shots of firewall rules) to back up questionnaire answers.
*   **Scalability:** Questionnaires are a cost-effective way to assess a large number of low-risk vendors.

## Real-World Examples
*   **Initial Assessment:** Sending a 50-question survey to a new SaaS provider to understand their data encryption practices.
*   **Annual Re-certification:** Requiring all vendors to update their security questionnaire responses once a year.
*   **Vendor Portal:** An online system where vendors can upload their answers and supporting evidence.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Self-assessment," "Survey," "Information gathering," "Evidence," "Scalability."
*   **Scenario:** To quickly assess 20 potential vendors, the security manager sends out a **Questionnaire**.
*   **Validation:** Questionnaires are useful but should be validated with audits for high-risk vendors.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Risk Identification:** Questionnaires help identify risks in the vendor’s environment that could affect the organization.
*   **Compliance:** Questionnaires often include sections on how the vendor complies with laws like HIPAA or GDPR.
