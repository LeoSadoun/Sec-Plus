## Official Definition
A formal statement by a third party or internal authority confirming that a set of security controls or processes meets specific requirements.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **AOC (Attestation of Compliance):** A document common in PCI DSS where a qualified professional (QSA) confirms the merchant is compliant.
*   **Audit vs. Attestation:** An audit is the process of examining evidence; an attestation is the formal conclusion or sign-off.
*   **Third-party Attestation:** Providing high levels of assurance because the reviewer is independent.
*   **Self-Attestation:** The organization itself claims it is compliant (Lower assurance).

## Real-World Examples
*   **SOC 2 Attestation:** A CPA firm signing off on a service provider’s security and availability controls.
*   **PCI DSS AOC:** A retailer submitting their annual compliance sign-off to their acquiring bank.
*   **Vendor Security Attestation:** A software vendor signing a document stating they follow secure coding practices.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Formal statement," "Sign-off," "Confirmation," "AOC," "Assurance."
*   **Scenario:** If a customer asks for proof that you are following security standards, you provide an **Attestation**.
*   **Independence:** The more independent the attester, the higher the trust in the document.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Third-party Risk:** Attestations are a key piece of evidence used during vendor assessment.
*   **Security Governance:** Attestations provide the "proof" that governance objectives are being met.
