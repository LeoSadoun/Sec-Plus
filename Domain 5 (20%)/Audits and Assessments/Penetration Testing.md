## Official Definition
A simulated cyberattack against an organization’s computer system, performed authorized and ethically, to find and exploit vulnerabilities.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Phases:** Planning (RoE) -> Reconnaissance -> Scanning/Vulnerability Research -> Exploitation -> Post-Exploitation -> Reporting.
*   **Black Box:** The tester has no prior knowledge of the system.
*   **White Box:** The tester has full knowledge (e.g., source code, network diagrams).
*   **Grey Box:** The tester has limited knowledge (e.g., a user account).
*   **Red Team vs. Blue Team:** Red attacks; Blue defends. Purple team facilitates communication between both.
*   **Persistence:** Maintaining access to the system after the initial breach.
*   **Pivot/Lateral Movement:** Moving from one compromised host to another within the network.

## Real-World Examples
*   **External Pen Test:** Attempting to breach the corporate network via the public-facing web server.
*   **Internal Pen Test:** Simulating an "insider threat" or a guest plugging a laptop into a conference room port.
*   **Social Engineering Test:** Sending "phishing" emails to employees to see who clicks.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Exploit," "Lateral Movement," "Persistence," "Black/Grey/White Box," "Red/Blue/Purple Team."
*   **Vulnerability Scan vs. Pen Test:** A scan *finds* flaws; a pen test *exploits* them to see how deep they go.
*   **Authorization:** Always ensure a pen test is authorized with a signed **Rules of Engagement**.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Risk Assessment:** Pen test results provide a realistic view of the organization’s risks.
*   **Security Awareness:** Social engineering pen tests are often used to identify where more training is needed.
