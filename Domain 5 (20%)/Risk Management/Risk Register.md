## Official Definition
A central document or database used to record and track identified risks, their severity, and the status of their mitigation efforts.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Risk ID:** A unique identifier for each risk.
*   **Description:** A clear explanation of the threat and vulnerability.
*   **Owner:** The person responsible for managing the risk.
*   **Current Status:** Whether the risk is being mitigated, accepted, etc.
*   **Residual Risk:** The risk remaining after controls have been implemented.
*   **Inherent Risk:** The risk level before any controls are applied.

## Real-World Examples
*   **Corporate Risk Dashboard:** A tool like Archer or a simple Excel sheet tracking all known security gaps.
*   **Audit Finding:** An auditor discovers an unmitigated risk that was not in the register, indicating a governance failure.
*   **Tracking Remediation:** Marking a risk as "Closed" once a critical patch has been deployed across the fleet.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Tracking," "Document," "Repository," "Owner," "Residual Risk."
*   **Scenario:** To provide management with a bird's-eye view of all organizational risks, you maintain a **Risk Register**.
*   **Residual vs. Inherent:** Residual is what's left *after* you do your job.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Audits (Domain 5):** Auditors use the risk register to verify that the organization is aware of and managing its threats.
*   **Vulnerability Management:** The risk register often tracks high-level risks, while the vulnerability scan report tracks technical flaws.
