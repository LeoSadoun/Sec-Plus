## Official Definition
**Risk Appetite:** The broad amount of risk an organization is willing to accept in pursuit of its goals.
**Risk Tolerance:** The specific, measurable level of variation an organization is willing to accept around its risk appetite.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Strategic Alignment:** Risk appetite is set by the Board/Executives.
*   **Thresholds:** Tolerance defines the boundaries (e.g., "We can tolerate up to 4 hours of downtime, but no more").
*   **Risk-Averse:** Low appetite for risk (e.g., a hospital or bank).
*   **Risk-Taking:** Higher appetite for risk (e.g., a tech startup).

## Real-World Examples
*   **Appetite:** A company decides to move all operations to the cloud to innovate, accepting the inherent risks of third-party hosting.
*   **Tolerance:** A Service Level Agreement (SLA) stating that a database must be available 99.9% of the time (0.1% downtime tolerance).
*   **Zero Tolerance:** A policy stating that there is no tolerance for data breaches involving customer PII.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Willingness," "Threshold," "Acceptance," "Variation," "Appetite (Broad)," "Tolerance (Specific)."
*   **Scenario:** If an executive says, "We are willing to lose $1M to enter this market," they are defining **Risk Appetite**. If they say, "We cannot exceed $1.1M in losses," they are defining **Tolerance**.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Risk Strategies:** If a risk exceeds the tolerance level, a strategy (Mitigate, Transfer, Avoid) must be applied.
*   **Governance:** The board’s risk appetite drives the entire security policy framework.
