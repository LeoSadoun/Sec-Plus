## Official Definition
The process of discovering, recognizing, and documenting the potential risks that could affect an organization's assets and operations.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Asset Identification:** Determining what needs protection (hardware, software, data, people).
*   **Threat Identification:** Identifying potential sources of harm (natural disasters, malicious actors, accidental errors).
*   **Vulnerability Identification:** Finding weaknesses in the environment (unpatched software, lack of physical security).
*   **Methods:** Brainstorming, interviews, surveys, automated scanning (vulnerability scanners), and reviewing historical logs.

## Real-World Examples
*   **Vulnerability Scanning:** Running Nessus or OpenVAS to identify unpatched systems.
*   **Threat Intelligence:** Reviewing CISA alerts to identify new malware targeting your industry.
*   **Asset Inventory:** Maintaining a CMDB (Configuration Management Database) to track all network devices.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Discover," "Inventory," "Scan," "Threat," "Vulnerability."
*   **Scenario:** The first step in a risk management program is identifying what assets the organization owns.
*   **Risk = Threat x Vulnerability:** Identification is about finding the variables in this equation.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Vulnerability Management (Domain 4):** Risk identification directly feeds into the vulnerability management lifecycle.
*   **Asset Management (Domain 4):** You cannot manage risk for assets you don't know you have.
