## Official Definition
The systematic process of evaluating the potential impact and likelihood of identified risks to prioritize them for treatment.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Qualitative Analysis:** Uses descriptive rankings (Low, Medium, High) based on expert judgment.
*   **Quantitative Analysis:** Uses numerical values and formulas to calculate financial impact.
*   **SLE (Single Loss Expectancy):** Asset Value x Exposure Factor (EF).
*   **ALE (Annualized Loss Expectancy):** SLE x ARO (Annualized Rate of Occurrence).
*   **Likelihood:** The probability that a threat will exploit a vulnerability.
*   **Impact:** The magnitude of harm if a risk is realized (Financial, Reputational, Operational).

## Real-World Examples
*   **Qualitative:** A spreadsheet where "Server Room Flood" is marked as "High Impact, Low Likelihood."
*   **Quantitative:** Calculating that a DDoS attack costs $10,000/hour (SLE) and happens twice a year (ARO), leading to an ALE of $20,000.
*   **NIST SP 800-30:** A widely used framework for conducting risk assessments.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "SLE," "ALE," "ARO," "Impact," "Likelihood," "Qualitative," "Quantitative."
*   **Calculation:** Be ready to calculate ALE. If SLE = $5,000 and ARO = 0.5, ALE = $2,500.
*   **Subjectivity:** Qualitative is faster but subjective; Quantitative is data-driven but time-consuming.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Control Selection:** The results of the assessment determine which security controls are worth the investment (ROI).
*   **Business Impact Analysis (BIA):** Assessment focuses on risks; BIA focuses on the consequences of those risks to business continuity.
