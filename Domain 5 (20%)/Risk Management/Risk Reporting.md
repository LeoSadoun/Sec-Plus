## Official Definition
The process of communicating risk assessment results, mitigation progress, and current risk posture to stakeholders and decision-makers.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Dashboards:** Visual representations of risk levels for quick review by executives.
*   **Heat Maps:** Grids showing the distribution of risks based on Impact and Likelihood.
*   **Frequency:** Regular intervals (monthly, quarterly) or triggered by significant events.
*   **Audience:** Tailoring the report to the audience (Technical vs. Executive).

## Real-World Examples
*   **Quarterly Risk Report:** A presentation to the Board of Directors showing that the number of "High" risks has decreased due to new security investments.
*   **Monthly Compliance Report:** Showing the percentage of systems that are fully patched.
*   **Ad-hoc Report:** Reporting on the potential impact of a new "Zero Day" vulnerability discovered in the wild.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Communication," "Stakeholders," "Dashboard," "Heat Map," "Posture."
*   **Executive View:** Executives want to see the "Big Picture" (financial and strategic impact), not technical CVE details.
*   **Consistency:** Reporting must be consistent to track trends over time.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Governance:** Reporting provides the oversight necessary for effective governance.
*   **Incident Response:** Major incidents should trigger immediate risk reporting to adjust the organization's posture.
