## Official Definition
The various methods an organization can choose to handle a risk once it has been identified and assessed.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Accept:** Acknowledging the risk but taking no action (usually because the cost of mitigation exceeds the impact).
*   **Avoid:** Eliminating the risk by stopping the activity that causes it (e.g., shutting down a vulnerable service).
*   **Mitigate:** Reducing the risk by implementing controls (e.g., installing a firewall, patching).
*   **Transfer:** Shifting the financial burden of the risk to a third party (e.g., buying cybersecurity insurance, outsourcing).

## Real-World Examples
*   **Accept:** Deciding not to patch a legacy system that is isolated and carries low-value data.
*   **Avoid:** Canceling a project to develop an app in a high-risk region due to theft concerns.
*   **Mitigate:** Implementing MFA to reduce the risk of account takeover.
*   **Transfer:** Purchasing a policy that covers the costs of forensics and notification after a breach.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Accept," "Avoid," "Mitigate," "Transfer," "Insurance," "Controls."
*   **Scenario:** If a company buys insurance, they are **Transferring** risk. If they stop using a specific software because it's too buggy, they are **Avoiding** risk.
*   **Cost-Benefit:** Mitigation is chosen when the cost of the control is less than the ALE.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Security Controls (Domain 1):** Mitigate is the strategy that directly leads to the selection of Technical, Administrative, and Physical controls.
*   **Third-Party Risk:** Outsourcing is often a form of risk transfer.
