## Official Definition
Step-by-step instructions for performing specific tasks or responding to specific events in a consistent and secure manner.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Mandatory:** Procedures ensure that critical tasks are performed correctly every time.
*   **SOP (Standard Operating Procedure):** Documentation of routine activities (e.g., onboarding a new user).
*   **Playbooks:** Detailed procedures for responding to specific security incidents (e.g., Ransomware Playbook).
*   **Checklists:** Often used within procedures to ensure no steps are missed.

## Real-World Examples
*   **User Onboarding:** A 10-step process involving HR approval, account creation in AD, and hardware provisioning.
*   **Incident Response:** Steps to isolate a compromised host from the network.
*   **Backup Restoration:** Precise steps to recover a database from an off-site backup.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Step-by-step," "Detailed," "How-to," "Consistency," "Routine."
*   **Scenario:** If a junior admin needs to know the exact steps to reset a firewall, they consult the **Procedures**.
*   **Level of Detail:** Procedures are the most granular level of documentation (Policy > Standard > Procedure).

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Operations (Domain 4):** Procedures are the "manual" for day-to-day security operations.
*   **Change Management:** Changes to systems should follow a defined procedure to minimize risk.
