## Official Definition
Specific, mandatory requirements or configurations that ensure consistency across the organization’s technology and security implementations.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Mandatory:** Like policies, standards must be followed to maintain compliance.
*   **Baseline:** A minimum level of security that must be met.
*   **Interoperability:** Standards ensure different systems can work together securely.
*   **Benchmarks:** Often derived from industry leaders like CIS (Center for Internet Security) or NIST.

## Real-World Examples
*   **Encryption Standard:** Requiring all data at rest to be encrypted using AES-256.
*   **OS Hardening Standard:** A specific list of services to disable and settings to enable on all Windows Server 11 instances.
*   **Wi-Fi Standard:** Mandating WPA3-Enterprise for all corporate wireless networks.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Mandatory," "Specific," "Configuration," "Baseline," "Consistency."
*   **Scenario:** If a company wants all web servers to be configured identically for security, they implement a **Standard**.
*   **Baseline vs. Standard:** A baseline is the *starting point* (minimum), while a standard is the *requirement* to reach or maintain it.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Architecture (Domain 2):** Standards dictate how the architecture should be built and hardened.
*   **Incident Response (Domain 4):** Standardized configurations make it easier to detect anomalies and unauthorized changes.
