## Official Definition
The organizational framework, including hierarchy and decision-making bodies, that directs and controls security activities.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Centralized vs. Decentralized:** Whether security decisions are made at headquarters or distributed across departments.
*   **Steering Committee:** A high-level group (executives, stakeholders) that aligns security goals with business objectives.
*   **Board of Directors:** Provides oversight and ensures security is treated as a business risk.
*   **CISO (Chief Information Security Officer):** The executive responsible for the security program.

## Real-World Examples
*   **Security Steering Committee:** Meeting monthly to review the budget for a new EDR solution.
*   **Reporting Structure:** The CISO reporting directly to the CEO or Board rather than the CIO (to avoid conflict of interest).
*   **Global Security Operations:** A large enterprise with regional security leads reporting to a Global CISO.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Decision-making," "Oversight," "Steering Committee," "CISO," "Alignment."
*   **Scenario:** To ensure security initiatives have executive support and funding, the organization establishes a **Steering Committee**.
*   **Strategic Alignment:** The primary goal of governance is to ensure security supports the business.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Risk Management:** Governance structures determine the organization's risk tolerance.
*   **Third-Party Risk:** The board or steering committee often reviews major vendor contracts for risk.
