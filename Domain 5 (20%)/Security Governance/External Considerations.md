## Official Definition
Factors outside of the organization’s direct control, such as laws, regulations, and industry standards, that influence its security posture.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Compliance:** Adhering to legal and regulatory requirements.
*   **Regulatory Environment:** Laws like HIPAA (Healthcare), PCI DSS (Credit Cards), or Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX - Financial).
*   **Threat Landscape:** The current state of cyber threats (e.g., rise in ransomware).
*   **Geopolitics:** International tensions that may increase the risk of nation-state attacks.
*   **Industry Standards:** Adopting frameworks like ISO 27001 or NIST CSF to meet customer expectations.

## Real-World Examples
*   **GDPR:** A US-based company must implement strict data protections if they serve EU citizens.
*   **PCI DSS:** Any retailer that accepts credit cards must comply with these technical and operational standards.
*   **Supply Chain:** Assessing the security of a software provider located in a high-risk geopolitical region.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Laws," "Regulations," "Compliance," "External," "Legal."
*   **Scenario:** If a company expands into a new country and must change its data storage practices, it is responding to **External Considerations**.
*   **Due Diligence:** Investigating these external factors before starting a new business venture.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Risk Assessment:** External threats and legal requirements are major inputs into the risk assessment process.
*   **Privacy (Compliance):** Privacy laws are a primary external driver for implementing technical privacy controls.
