## Official Definition
Clearly defined duties and expectations for individuals within an organization to ensure accountability and effective security operations.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Data Owner/Executive:** Ultimately responsible for the security and use of a specific data set (often a senior manager).
*   **Data Steward/Custodian:** Responsible for the technical implementation of security controls (e.g., IT staff, DBAs).
*   **Privacy Officer:** Ensures the organization complies with privacy laws (e.g., GDPR).
*   **System Owner:** Responsible for the overall security of a specific system.
*   **Users:** Responsible for following policies and reporting anomalies.

## Real-World Examples
*   **Data Owner:** The Head of HR is the owner of employee payroll data.
*   **Data Custodian:** A DBA setting up encryption and access controls for the HR database.
*   **Privacy Officer:** Reviewing a new marketing campaign to ensure it doesn't violate customer privacy rights.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Accountability," "Owner," "Custodian," "Steward," "Responsibility."
*   **Data Owner vs. Custodian:** The Owner decides *who* gets access; the Custodian *grants* it.
*   **Ultimate Responsibility:** The Data Owner (often an executive) is the one "whose head is on the block" if things go wrong.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Identity and Access Management (Domain 4):** Roles and responsibilities define who has the authority to approve access.
*   **Auditing:** Clearly defined roles ensure that auditors can trace actions to specific individuals (Accountability).
