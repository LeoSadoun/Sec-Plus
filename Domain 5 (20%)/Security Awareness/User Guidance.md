## Official Definition
Clear, actionable advice provided to users to help them maintain security in their daily tasks and protect organizational assets.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Job-Aids:** Small, easily accessible reminders (e.g., mousepads with the helpdesk number, posters about password safety).
*   **Onboarding Training:** The initial security training every employee receives when they join.
*   **Refresher Training:** Periodic (often annual) updates to keep security top-of-mind.
*   **Role-based Training:** Specific guidance tailored to a user’s job (e.g., secure coding for devs, social engineering for HR).
*   **Executive Support:** When leaders model secure behavior, it reinforces the guidance for all staff.

## Real-World Examples
*   **Password Manager:** Providing a corporate password manager and training users on how to generate strong, unique passwords.
*   **Clean Desk Guide:** Providing a checklist of what should be locked away at the end of every day.
*   **Travel Guidance:** Advising employees on how to use VPNs and avoid "Juice Jacking" when traveling for business.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Actionable," "Advice," "Training," "Job-Aids," "Refresher."
*   **Scenario:** A developer receives specific training on how to avoid OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities. This is **Role-based User Guidance**.
*   **Goal:** Guidance should be simple, practical, and easy to follow.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Policies:** User guidance is the "translation" of complex security policies into simple instructions for employees.
*   **Social Engineering (Domain 2):** Guidance is the primary defense against social engineering tactics.
