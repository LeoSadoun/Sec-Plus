## Official Definition
Educational programs designed to help employees recognize and respond to phishing attacks, reducing the risk of credential theft and malware infection.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Phishing Simulators:** Tools that send "fake" phishing emails to track who clicks, who enters credentials, and who reports.
*   **Spear Phishing:** Targeted attacks against specific individuals or departments.
*   **Whaling:** Targeted attacks against high-level executives (CEO, CFO).
*   **Vishing:** Phishing via voice calls (VoIP).
*   **Smishing:** Phishing via SMS/text messages.
*   **Reporting Button:** A "one-click" tool in email clients for users to report suspicious messages.

## Real-World Examples
*   **Monthly Simulation:** Sending a fake "IT Password Reset" email to all staff.
*   **Follow-up Training:** Automatically assigning a 5-minute video to any user who clicks a link in a simulation.
*   **Gamification:** Rewarding departments that have the highest reporting rates and lowest click rates.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Simulation," "Report," "Credential Theft," "Spear Phishing," "Vishing/Smishing."
*   **Scenario:** If an employee receives an email that looks like it's from the CEO asking for gift cards, they are being targeted by **Whaling**.
*   **Culture:** The goal is to move from a "culture of clicking" to a "culture of reporting."

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Threat Actors (Domain 2):** Phishing is the #1 vector for initial access by threat actors.
*   **Incident Response:** User reports are often the first alert an IR team receives about an active campaign.
