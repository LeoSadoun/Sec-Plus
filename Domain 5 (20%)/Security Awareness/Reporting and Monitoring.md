## Official Definition
The mechanisms by which users report suspicious activity and the process for tracking the effectiveness of the security awareness program.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Reporting Channels:** Email (e.g., abuse@company.com), phone (Security Hotline), or web portal.
*   **No-Blame Culture:** Encouraging users to report even if they made a mistake (e.g., "I clicked the link, I'm sorry").
*   **Metrics (KPIs):**
    *   **Click Rate:** % of users who fail a phishing simulation.
    *   **Reporting Rate:** % of users who report a suspicious email.
    *   **Mean Time to Report:** How quickly users report a threat after it lands in their inbox.
*   **Feedback Loop:** Telling the user "Thank you, you were right!" after they report something to encourage future behavior.

## Real-World Examples
*   **Phish Alert Button:** A button in Outlook that automatically forwards the email to the SOC and deletes it from the inbox.
*   **Security Dashboard:** Showing management that reporting rates have increased by 20% since the new training program started.
*   **Incident Trigger:** A user reports a suspicious phone call, leading the security team to block the attacker’s IP range.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Metrics," "KPI," "Feedback," "Reporting Button," "No-Blame," "Culture."
*   **Scenario:** To measure if security training is working, the CISO tracks the **Reporting Rate** of suspicious emails.
*   **Positive Reinforcement:** Users are more likely to report if they feel supported rather than punished.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Incident Response:** User reporting is one of the most effective "detective controls" an organization has.
*   **Compliance:** Many regulations require organizations to have a clear mechanism for reporting security concerns.
