## Official Definition
The right of individuals to control how their personal information is collected, used, shared, and stored.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **PII (Personally Identifiable Information):** Any data that can be used to identify an individual (Name, SSN, IP address).
*   **PHI (Protected Health Information):** Health-related data protected by laws like HIPAA.
*   **Data Minimization:** Collecting only the data that is absolutely necessary for the task.
*   **Right to be Forgotten:** The legal right in some jurisdictions (GDPR) for individuals to have their data deleted.
*   **Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA):** An analysis of how a new system or process will affect individual privacy.

## Real-World Examples
*   **GDPR:** Implementing "Cookie Banners" and allowing users to opt-out of tracking.
*   **HIPAA:** Ensuring that doctors only see the records of patients they are actively treating.
*   **Anonymization:** Removing names and addresses from a dataset before using it for research.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "PII," "PHI," "GDPR," "Minimization," "Consent," "Assessment."
*   **Scenario:** If a company asks for your birthday only to verify you are over 18, but doesn't store it, they are practicing **Data Minimization**.
*   **Privacy vs. Security:** Security is about *protecting* the data; Privacy is about *how the data should be used*.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Data Protection (Domain 3):** Technical controls like encryption and masking are used to enforce privacy policies.
*   **Governance:** The organization's Privacy Policy is a foundational governance document.
