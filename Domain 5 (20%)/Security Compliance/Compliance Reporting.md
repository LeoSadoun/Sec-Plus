## Official Definition
The process of documenting and communicating how an organization is meeting its legal, regulatory, and internal security requirements.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Internal vs. External:** Reporting to management vs. reporting to regulators or customers.
*   **Evidence Collection:** Gathering logs, policies, and audit results to prove compliance.
*   **Frequency:** Monthly status updates or annual regulatory filings.
*   **Executive Summary:** A high-level view for leadership, often highlighting "Red/Amber/Green" status.

## Real-World Examples
*   **Annual PCI DSS Attestation:** Filing an Attestation of Compliance (AoC) with a credit card processor.
*   **Board Report:** Showing a slide with the percentage of employees who have completed annual security training.
*   **Audit Summary:** A document provided to a potential customer showing that the company has passed its latest SOC 2 audit.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Documentation," "Communication," "Evidence," "Regulatory," "Filing."
*   **Scenario:** To prove to the federal government that healthcare data is protected, a hospital submits a **Compliance Report**.
*   **Goal:** The primary goal of compliance reporting is to provide transparency and accountability.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Auditing:** Audit findings are the primary data source for compliance reports.
*   **Security Governance:** Governance defines the reporting requirements and frequency.
