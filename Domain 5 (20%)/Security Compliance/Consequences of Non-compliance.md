## Official Definition
The negative impacts an organization faces when it fails to adhere to legal, regulatory, or contractual security requirements.

## Key Concepts/Technical Details
*   **Fines:** Monetary penalties levied by regulators (e.g., GDPR fines can be millions of Euros).
*   **Legal Action:** Lawsuits from customers, partners, or the government.
*   **Reputational Damage:** Loss of customer trust and brand value.
*   **Loss of License:** Being banned from certain markets (e.g., losing the ability to process credit cards if PCI DSS fails).
*   **Imprisonment:** In extreme cases, executives can face jail time (e.g., SOX violations).

## Real-World Examples
*   **Equifax Breach:** Facing billions in settlements and fines after failing to patch a known vulnerability.
*   **GDPR Fine:** A company being fined 4% of its annual turnover for failing to protect EU citizen data.
*   **Vendor Termination:** A client canceling a multimillion-dollar contract because the vendor failed a security audit.

## Exam Tips
*   **Keywords:** "Fines," "Lawsuits," "Reputation," "License," "Sanctions."
*   **Scenario:** If a company fails to protect credit card data, the most immediate consequence might be a **Loss of the ability to process payments**.
*   **Risk Management:** The severity of these consequences is a major factor in determining risk appetite.

## Relationship to other concepts
*   **Risk Assessment:** The "Impact" section of a risk assessment often includes these potential consequences.
*   **Security Governance:** Policies are designed specifically to avoid these negative outcomes.
